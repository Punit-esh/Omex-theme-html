Forbes

As the demand for new applications to run businesses effectively continues to grow, the time it takes for an application to evolve is still enormous. The slow go-to-market strategy of large enterprises is driven by complexity, which results in a prolonged launch cycle. Moreover, many researchers suggest that the software development challenges for 2021 will undoubtedly carry into 2022.

In the middle of a "developer drought," recruiting effective software development talent is a challenge. Hence, businesses are beginning to take an alternative path with Low-code and NoCode solutions.

The mixture of low-code and conventional innovation is projected to be adopted by 75% of businesses. By 2030, the global low-code development platform market is expected to produce $187 billion in revenue. It will account for more than 65% of application development activity by 2024.

Almost 60% of all custom apps are now developed outside of the IT department. The majority of these users (40%) work in a professional setting.

Low-code/no-code tools are enabling non-programmers, or really anybody, to develop business workflow applications. This is also enabling users to create solutions that reflect specific data inquiries and workflow processes; their low-code/no-code-crafted programs can also easily be integrated into larger business processes.
